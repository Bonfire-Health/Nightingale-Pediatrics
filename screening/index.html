Questions About ADHD Screening

Is this a full ADHD Diagnosis?

No. This is a clinical screening service. It identifies if your child presents with factors that might warrant a full diagnostic assessment. Families often use the screening report for initial school discussions.

How does the "Computerized Neuropsychological Test" work?

It's a standardized tool administered remotely on a computer. It measures reaction time, sustained attention, and impulse control with built-in visual and auditory distractors. The child demonstrates attention and executive functioning skills in real time to simulate other environments, like a school classroom.

What happens after the report?

If attention factors are indicated, we can explore Parent Coaching to build skills for your child, or a full diagnostic test if you require exam accommodations or want a formal diagnosis.
